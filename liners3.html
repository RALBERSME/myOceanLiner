Welcome on board of RMS Lusitania!!


We warmly welcome you to the RMS Lusitania,
which is in the service of the British Cunard Line shipping company. Today, Saturday, May 1st, 1915, we will leave New York at 12:20 p.m. with 1,258 passengers and 701 crew members (a total of 1,367 men, 463 women and 129 children) heading for Liverpool.

You will all arrive safely in Liverpool on May 7th.

We still have an hour before the ship sets sail. That is enough time to briefly introduce yourself to your fellow passengers, to make friends on board, but also to take a closer look at our wonderful ship.
We are a ship in a class of its own. You can just scroll down or use the buttons.

Representatives of the crew introduce themselves:

W.T.Turner


I am Captain William Thomas Turner.

I always like to go to Liverpool, because I was born there in 1856. So I'm English and currently 58 years old.
My father was a captain. I've been with the Cunard Line since 1878. I received my captain's license in 1886 and first sailed in the Mediterranean.

Despite my good nautical skills, I don't fit the ideal image of a Cunard captain. The shipping company expects me to entertain the first class passengers. But I can't constantly surround myself with "a bunch of damn monkeys who are always chattering". Even if I seem brusque, I still understand the needs of my passengers and I take them seriously.
I steered the Lusitania in 1908, and in 1910 I switched to its sister ship, the Mauretania, and covered the Liverpool - New York route in just twelve days. That was a new record. I've been a commodore since 1913. I then commanded the maiden voyage of the Aquitania. I have two sons (Percy Wilfred and Norman Henry), but my marriage broke up and my wife Alice moved away with the children.

As an experienced captain, I am delighted to welcome you on board.
I am now getting everything ready for takeoff. I will see you in the saloon for dinner.

Here is a picture of me on the ship's bridge.

G.W.Beauchamp

I am George William Beauchamp.

I was born in 1912 in Totton / UK. On leaving school I went to sea. I served on the Union Castle, the Royal Mail, and the White Star Lines. At the age of 24 I signed on to the Titanic as a stoker. Luckily I survived the disaster.
I escaped in Lifeboat 13, and was a witness at the British Board of Trade Inquiry into the loss of the Titanic.

I told the enquiry that I stood on the deck of the Titanic by the lifeboat and helped the ladies and children who were there to get in. Then the officer gave the order 'Lower the boat, that's enough. Who can row?' I said, 'I can.' He said, 'Get in the boat. Lower the boat. That's enough.' We were under the engine room hold. Everything went down easily until the boat reached the bottom, up to the hold, then we had difficulties keeping it away from the side of the ship. The Titanic went down bow first. You could hear the screams after the ship went down. It was terrible. We rowed with all our might which was not easy, but we kept rowing until we saw the light of the Carpathia, another ship of the Cunard Line. The Carpathia saved us. We as surviving crew members were brought back to England and after giving evidence to White Star officials, we were taken back to Southampton.
I have remained loyal to the shipping and am delighted to be working as a stoker on this magnificent ship today. I wish everyone a safe journey. But now I must get to work, otherwise we cannot set sail at 12:20.

Mrs. Jolivet

I am delighted to be here today. Most people know me from the stage. My name is Marguerite Lucile Jolivet, but you can all call me Rita.

I am 25 and a British actress of French ancestry. I am traveling aboard Lusitania to see my brother Alfred before he goes to fight in World War I. I have booked my ticket, D 1350, just this morning and haven`t anticipated to meet my brother-in-law George Vernon nor his mentor Charles Frohman aboard this ship. Mrs. Carson

My name is Henrietta McCulloch Carson, but you can call me "Etta".

I come from Scotland. My father James works as a coachman (domestic). I studied at Dumfries Academy in Scotland. I left Glasgow in 1911 as a second cabin passenger on the steamship Scotian of the Allan Line, and came to Canada where her sister (Mary) lived at the time (in Selkirk, Manitoba). At the age of 17 I was a student intending to work as a teacher in Canada and belonged to the Presbyterian Church.
My father had passed away in February or March 1915, and I am now going to Scotland to see my family there. My mother had made arrangements for me to sail home on the Grampian. However my older brother George convinced me to change ships and to sail instead on the Lusitania thinking it would be safer because it is faster. That's why I'm on board today and am looking forward to the crossing and arrival in England together with you.

Mr. Harris

Hello! Do you know Dwight Carlton Harris?

But you should, because that's me. As you can see, am 5'11 ½” tall, have blue eyes, long nose, “light and brown” hair, ruddy complexion and an oval face. I was born in New York City on January 6, 1884 as a son of a banker who died in 1903 in Manhattan. I don't have any brothers or sisters. I was an uncle of Henry B. Harris, who was lost on the Titanic. That is why I have a special life jacket made for myself from the sporting company Abercrombie and Fitch. It has special pockets for storing valuables and provided more safety than the standard ones. With this life jacket I really feel safe on this ship. LOL, ok just joking. If I didn't trust this ship, I wouldn't have boarded it at all. As a theatrical manager, I am on my way to Europe on a trip in connection with my theatrical business. I look forward to getting to know many of you better this evening in the drawing room over dinner. Have a good trip, everyone.
Here is a picture of me for your photo album.

Baby Audrey

Hello, I'mAudrey Pearl

and because I'm only 3 months old and can't speak yet, my nanny Alice will speak for me. I am the 4th child of Surgeon-Major F. Warren Pearl and Amy Lea Duncan Pearl. Shortly after my birth, my father was instructed to report to the American Embassy in London. So we are now on our way to London. My daddy is a veteran of the Spanish-American war. Because of the fact that he is a sharp army officer he has given very specific emergency instructions to my mum and our two nannies how to react in emergency cases. My nanny Alice loves me very much and always takes good care of me and my little brother Stuart. I feel very comfortable with her. It's a shame that I'm still so small and don't get to see much of the beautiful ship and the exciting journey. I'm hungry again. Where can I get milk?
