Concluding considerations and documents

The sinking of the Lusitania, caused by a torpedo from a German submarine, resulted in the deaths of 128 American citizens. This generated massive public outrage in the United States and increased pressure on then-US President Woodrow Wilson to join the war. The sinking of the Lusitania thus contributed significantly to the United States' entry into the war. The influence of the sinking of the Lusitania on the outcome of the war should therefore not be underestimated.
Before the Lusitania sank, she sent a distress call by radio. The Peel 11 “The Wanderer” fishing boat from the Isle of Man was able to rescue between 160 and 200 people. The rescued people were brought ashore in Queenstown. The people of Queenstown (Ireland) extended sacrificial hospitality and aid to the many survivors. Many of the survivors suffered from post-traumatic stress syndrome.

United States

There was debate across the United States about how Americans should respond. Former President Teddy Roosevelt advocated military retaliation and entry into World War I.
