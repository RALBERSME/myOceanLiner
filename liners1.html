The industrial revolution had led to a sharp increase in intercontinental trade since the 19th century. Good and stable shipping was needed to ensure this. The invention of steamships made it possible to sail independently of the wind. Ships became larger, more robust and faster. A competition began which was honoured with the award of the Blue Riband of the Atlantic. This is an award given to the fastest ship for passengers on the transatlantic route Europe - New York and was given from around the 1860s. Well-known winners are among others the Etruria and the Umbria (both in service from 1885 with speeds of around 18 to 19 knots). And also the Kaiser Wilhelm der Gro√üe (1897). The British sister ships Lusitania and Mauretania were commissioned from 1907 and were able to defend the Blue Riband until 1929. And many other ships received this award... Rumor has it that the Titanic was also keen to set a new record and win the Blue Riband on her maiden voyage.
